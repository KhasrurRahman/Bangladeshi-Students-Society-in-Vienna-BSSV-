If you hold a valid Bangladeshi driving licence and now live in Austria, you can apply to convert it to an Austrian licence. Bangladesh is not one of the countries exempt from the practical test, so the process normally includes a medical certificate and a practical driving examination.

This guide is based on the current information from [ÖAMTC](https://www.oeamtc.at/thema/fuehrerschein/auslaendischer-eu-und-ewr-fuehrerschein-in-oesterreich-16179194), the [Austrian government](https://www.oesterreich.gv.at/en/themen/persoenliche_dokumente_und_bestaetigungen/fuehrerschein/3/Seite.040500) and the [Vienna Traffic Department](https://www.polizei.gv.at/wien/buergerservice/fuehrerschein/4/umschreibung.html). Requirements can differ between authorities, so confirm your document list before your appointment.

## Can you drive in Austria with a Bangladeshi licence?

The general rules for non-EEA licences are:

- If you do not have a residence in Austria, a recognised foreign licence can normally be used for up to 12 months from entry into Austria, provided you are at least 18.
- Once you establish residence in Austria, a non-EEA licence is normally valid for six months from the date you register that residence. After six months, you may no longer drive with it in Austria.
- Your Bangladeshi licence must also remain valid under Bangladeshi law.
- If the licence is not in German and its categories do not correspond clearly to classes A to E, carry either an international driving permit issued in Bangladesh or a translation that explains the scope of the licence.

The six month rule is a deadline for using the foreign licence in Austria. Submitting a conversion application does not automatically extend your right to drive.

## What applies specifically to Bangladesh?

Bangladesh does not appear on Austria's current exemption lists for conversion without a test. A Bangladeshi licence holder therefore normally needs:

- a medical certificate from an authorised doctor; and
- a practical test for every licence category being converted.

A theory test is not normally required for this conversion. The practical test must be taken in a driving school vehicle. If you take the test in an automatic vehicle, the Austrian licence may be restricted to automatic vehicles.

## Eligibility and proof of residence in Bangladesh

The authority may ask you to prove that, when the Bangladeshi licence was issued, you had your main residence in Bangladesh or stayed there for at least six months.

This proof is generally not required if you are a Bangladeshi citizen and already held the Bangladeshi licence when you established residence in Austria. The authority can still request further evidence in an individual case.

## Documents required in Vienna

Prepare the following:

- completed driving licence application, called a `Führerscheinantrag`;
- medical certificate, called an `ärztliches Gutachten`, from an authorised doctor and no more than 18 months old;
- original Bangladeshi driving licence;
- translation or ÖAMTC classification, if required by the authority;
- original passport and two copies, including the photo and visa pages;
- residence permit, if applicable;
- original residence registration form, called a `Meldezettel`, and one copy;
- one recent passport photo, normally 35 x 45 mm; and
- documents from the driving school for the practical test.

The Vienna authority may ask for other documents or extra copies. Take all originals with you.

## Classification and translation are not the same

An ÖAMTC classification maps the categories on a foreign licence to the corresponding European licence classes. According to ÖAMTC, the classification currently costs EUR 27.40 for non-members and is free for members.

If the licence is not written in Latin script or the German alphabet, you may also need a translation by a court-sworn translator. An ÖAMTC classification does not always replace that translation. Ask the responsible driving licence authority which document it will accept before visiting ÖAMTC or paying a translator.

## Step-by-step process

### 1. Confirm the documents with the authority

Contact the driving licence authority before arranging a translation or classification. Ask whether it accepts an ÖAMTC classification for your licence and whether a court-sworn translation is also required.

### 2. Obtain the classification or translation

If the authority requests an ÖAMTC classification, you can visit an ÖAMTC service centre. One Vienna option is:

**ÖAMTC Stützpunkt Wienerberg**<br />
Wienerbergstraße 27c<br />
1100 Vienna<br />
[Open in Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Wienerbergstra%C3%9Fe+27c,+1100+Wien,+Austria)

Not every licence can be classified immediately. If ÖAMTC cannot read or classify it, you may need a court-sworn translator.

### 3. Complete the medical examination

Book an examination with a doctor authorised to issue driving licence medical certificates. The certificate must be no more than 18 months old when used for the application.

One option previously used by community members is:

**DDr. Dipl.-Ing. M. Bermann**<br />
Pelzgasse 7/7<br />
1150 Vienna<br />
Telephone: +43 1 982 75 88<br />
[Open in Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Pelzgasse+7%2F7,+1150+Wien,+Austria)

Call first to confirm availability, authorisation and the current fee. Medical examination fees can change.

### 4. Register with a driving school

You need a driving school to prepare the practical-test documents and provide the examination vehicle. Ask for a written quote covering registration, any required driving lessons, the examination vehicle and test-related fees.

Community reports have mentioned about EUR 160 for initial driving school registration and roughly EUR 300 to EUR 400 for test registration plus two driving hours. These are personal estimates, not fixed official fees. Prices and the number of lessons vary.

### 5. Submit the application

Attend your appointment with all originals and copies. The authority checks the validity and authenticity of the Bangladeshi licence and whether anything prevents the issue of an Austrian licence.

Official guidance says these checks can take several weeks and may take longer when further enquiries are necessary. Do not rely on a fixed three to six month processing time. Ask the authority about your case.

### 6. Take the practical test

The practical test is required for a Bangladeshi licence conversion. It must be taken for every category you want to convert. Coordinate the appointment and vehicle with your driving school.

After you pass, the authority can issue a temporary driving licence while the Austrian card is produced. This temporary licence is valid for no more than four weeks and only within Austria.

### 7. Receive the Austrian licence

The authority keeps the non-EEA licence as part of the exchange. You can collect the Austrian card from the authority or arrange postal delivery where available.

The official exchange fee is currently EUR 90, excluding medical, classification, translation, driving school and test fees.

## Important warnings

- Do not drive after your foreign licence has ceased to be valid in Austria.
- A translation, classification, apostille or international driving permit does not extend the six month residence deadline.
- If you drive without a valid licence and cause an accident, the liability insurer may seek reimbursement from the driver or vehicle owner. The ÖAMTC leaflet states that this may be up to EUR 11,000.
- If you lend your vehicle to someone, make sure that person holds a valid licence for it.
- A separate moped licence is required to ride a moped unless another valid category covers it.

## Related note: registering a vehicle in Austria

The ÖAMTC information supplied with this guide also covers vehicle registration. A vehicle can normally be registered only by a person whose main residence is in Austria. Registration is handled by an insurance company's vehicle registration office for the district of your main residence.

The usual documents include proof of ownership, confirmation of Austrian liability insurance, an official photo ID and a valid roadworthiness inspection report, known as the Section 57a `Pickerl`, where required. The leaflet gives an approximate registration cost of EUR 265, but current charges should be confirmed with the registration office.

If you move to another political district, the vehicle may have to be registered again with the office responsible for the new district. Inform your insurance company whenever your address changes.
